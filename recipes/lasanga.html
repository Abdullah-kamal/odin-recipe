Lasagna, also known by the plural form lasagne, is a type of pasta made in wide, flat sheets.

It originates in Italian cuisine, where it is served in a number of ways ,
including in broth (lasagne in brodo), but is best known for its use in a
baked dish made by stacking layers of pasta, alternating with fillings such
as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses
(which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan), and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets
  • 1 jar pasta sauce (red sauce)
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Optional: cooked minced chicken/beef or veggies

How to make:

  1. Make the filling
    Mix together in a bowl:
    Pasta sauce
    Cooked minced meat or chopped veggies

  2. Layer

    In a baking dish:
    1. A little sauce
    2. Lasagna sheet
    3. Sauce
    4. Cheese

      5. Repeat until full.

  3. Top
    Add a final thick layer of cheese.
  4. Bake
    Bake at 180°C for 25–30 minutes until cheese melts and browns.
